The Importance of Shabbat

Shabbat is the Jewish day of rest. It starts at sunset on Friday and ends at nightfall on Saturday, offering a time to connect spiritually and with family.

Shabbat Teachings and Jewish Culture

Shabbat's Role in Jewish Life

Shabbat is not just a day of rest, but a way to reset spiritually, connect with community, and deepen one’s relationship with God. Here, we explore the various customs, prayers, and rituals observed during Shabbat.

The Importance of Family

Family is at the center of Jewish life, and Shabbat is a time when families gather to share meals, talk, and celebrate together. This day provides an opportunity to strengthen bonds and reflect on the week’s blessings.
